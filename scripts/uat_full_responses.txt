Query 2: What are the GFR 2017 tiered limits for direct laptop purchase from GeM without a traditional tender?
Role: government_officer | Grade: Pass
Response:
**Direct GeM Purchase without Tender (Rule 149)**

Yes, government departments in India can procure laptops directly through the Government e-Marketplace (GeM) without a traditional tender using these GFR 149 tiered limits:
- **Direct Purchase (Up to ₹50,000):** Direct purchase from any available seller on GeM without comparing quotes.
- **L1 Comparison (₹50,000 to ₹10,00,000):** Direct purchase through L1 comparison of at least three different manufacturers/brands on the portal.
- **Proprietary Article Certificate (PAC):** Single-brand laptop procurement is allowed on GeM only by uploading a PAC (Proprietary Article Certificate) signed by the competent authority.

Query 8: What is the difference between Limited Tender and Open Tender under CG Store Purchase Rules?
Role: government_officer | Grade: Pass
Response:
**Rule 162 (Limited Tender Enquiry) Guidelines**

- Limited Tender Enquiry may be adopted when the estimated value of goods to be procured is up to Rupees Fifty Lakhs.
- Copies of bidding documents should be sent directly by speed post/registered post/courier/e-mail to firms on the list of registered suppliers for the goods in question.
- The number of supplier firms in Limited Tender Enquiry should be more than three.
- Efforts should be made to identify a higher number of approved suppliers to obtain more responsive bids on competitive basis.

**Rule 162 (Limited Tender Enquiry) Additional Guidelines**

- Purchase through Limited Tender Enquiry may also be adopted even where the estimated value is more than Rupees Fifty Lakhs, if the competent authority certifies that the demand is urgent and any additional expenditure involved by not procuring through advertised tender enquiry is justified in view of urgency.
- The Ministry or Department should put on record the nature of the urgency and reasons why the procurement could not be anticipated.

Query 9: When is a department allowed to use the Single Tender method of procurement?
Role: government_officer | Grade: Pass
Response:
**Rule 166 Single Tender Enquiry Guidelines**

• Procurement from a single source may be resorted to in three circumstances:
 • Only a particular firm manufactures the required goods.
 • In an emergency, the required goods must be purchased from a specific source, and the reason for this decision must be recorded with approval from competent authority.
 • For standardization of machinery or spare parts compatible with existing equipment (advised by a technical expert and approved by competent authority), purchase only from a selected firm.

• Before procuring goods from a single source under sub-Rule 166(i) or 166(iii), provide the Proprietary Article Certificate in the following format:
 • The indented goods are manufactured by M/s. [Name].
 • No other make or model is acceptable for the following reasons: [Reasons].
