### B01: Purchase start karne se pehle administrative approval aur financial sanction mein kya difference hai?

**Latency:** 2021ms | **Tokens:** 375

**Sarvam 30B Answer:**

**Administrative Approval vs Financial Sanction**

**Administrative Approval** is the procedural clearance to initiate procurement process. It involves verifying compliance with procurement rules, checking eligibility of vendors, and ensuring proper documentation. This is typically done by departmental officers and is mandatory before any financial commitment.

**Financial Sanction** is the actual budget approval for the procurement. It involves allocating funds from the department's budget and ensuring availability of funds. This is done by the financial authority and is required before final award of contract.

**Key Differences:**
- Administrative approval focuses on process compliance and vendor eligibility
- Financial sanction focuses on budget availability and fund allocation
- Both are required but serve different purposes in the procurement lifecycle
- Administrative approval can be obtained first, followed by financial sanction

CG SPR 2002 emphasizes that administrative approval must precede financial sanction in all procurement processes.

### C03: How can specifications be written so that they do not favour one vendor?

**Latency:** 7870ms | **Tokens:** 1821

**Sarvam 30B Answer:**

**Writing Fair and Non-Discriminatory Specifications**

To ensure specifications do not favour one vendor, they must be written based on objective, measurable, and performance-based criteria, not on brand, model, or proprietary technology.

*   **Focus on Performance:** Define what the item must do, not how it must be made. For example, specify "cooling capacity of 5 tons" instead of "Brand X 5-ton AC."
*   **Use Measurable Criteria:** Include quantifiable parameters like "minimum 10-hour battery life," "energy efficiency rating of 3-star," or "must meet IS 14540:2016 standard."
*   **Avoid Prescriptive/Proprietary Specs:** Do not mandate a specific brand or model. Instead, state functional requirements. For example, for a computer, specify "16GB RAM, 512GB SSD, Windows 11 compatible" rather than "Must be a Dell Latitude 5420."

This approach aligns with the principles of **CG Store Purchase Rules (CG SPR) 2002** for objective tender evaluation and **General Financial Rules (GFR) 2017** for fair and transparent procurement. This ensures all qualified vendors can compete on an equal footing.

• **विशेषीकृत सेवाएं** - जैसे परामर्श, रखरखाव, या तकनीकी सहायता सेवाएं
• **सरकारी मानक** - जब विभाग के अपने अनुभव मानदंड हों

**CG SPR 2002 के अनुसार:**
- **Rule 4.3.1**: सिंगल टेंडर में ₹50,000 से ऊपर के लिए अनुभव अनिवार्य
- **Rule 4.3.2**: लिमिटेड टेंडर में ₹50,001-3,00,000 के लिए 3 निर्माताओं से अनुभव मांग सकते हैं
- **Rule 4.3.3**: ओपन टेंडर में ₹3,00,000 से ऊपर के लिए अनुभव आवश्यक

**GeM के लिए:**
- **L1 तुलना**: ₹25,001-5,00,000 के लिए अनुभव अनिवार्य
- **₹5,00,000 से ऊपर**: रिवर्स ऑक्शन अनिवार्य, अनुभव मानदंड जोड़ सकते हैं

**GFR 2017**: Rule 154 के अनुसार ₹25,000 तक अनुभव नहीं, ₹25,000-2,50,000 के लिए स्थानीय खरीद समिति।

**मुख्य बिंदु**: अनुभव की आवश्यकता तकनीकी जटिलता, जोखिम और कार्य की प्रकृति के आधार पर तय की जाती है, न कि मनमाने ढंग से।

### D05: Can tender conditions be changed after bids have already been opened?

**Latency:** 8236ms | **Tokens:** 1529

**Sarvam 30B Answer:**

**Tender Condition Changes After Bid Opening**

No, tender conditions cannot be changed after bids have been opened. This is a strict principle under the Chhattisgarh Store Purchase Rules (CG SPR) 2002 to ensure a fair and transparent process.

*   **Finality of Terms:** Once the bid opening process begins, the original tender conditions, including eligibility criteria, technical specifications, and financial requirements, are final and binding.
*   **Permitted Action:** The only action permitted is to seek *clarifications* from all bidders to ensure they have understood the original conditions correctly.
*   **Prohibition:** Any substantive change to the tender terms after bid opening is strictly prohibited. This rule is part of the general principles of CG SPR 2002, which govern the entire tender process to maintain a level playing field.
